Through autumn, the central gulf coast sees its strongest onshore flow in the
late afternoon. Mornings are typically calm, with the sea breeze building after
midday. Sheltered pockets on the lee side of points and jetties hold up best
when the prevailing wind is up, and they are the first places to settle when it
drops in the evening.

Water clarity swings with recent weather: a run of settled days clears things
up, while a strong onshore blow stirs the shallows. Plan around the forecast
window rather than a single day's outlook, because conditions in the shoulder
season change quickly from one day to the next.

Access is easiest from the public launches near the main channel; the outer
flats are shallow and reward a slow, careful approach at higher water.
